# Repository Goal

Create a practical framework for love, loyalty, trust, sexuality, family, friendship, boundaries, forgiveness, grief, and relational repair downstream of Ethosism. The Fidelity Framework applies objective reality, reciprocity, integrity, long-term responsibility, and contribution to the bonds by which people bind themselves to one another.

# Writing Direction

When writing or revising this book, follow [docs/writing-guide.md](docs/writing-guide.md). Keep the Fidelity Framework grounded in observable reality, reciprocal moral reasoning, embodied responsibility, trustworthy conduct, protection of the vulnerable, truthful repair, and decade-scale fidelity across friendship, family, marriage, sexuality, caregiving, and community.

The work should be secular and non-theological: it may draw respectfully from religious, philosophical, scientific, therapeutic, civic, and practical traditions, but it must not depend on revelation, clergy, supernatural claims, partisan identity, purity ideology, sexual libertinism, or any single theology for its authority.

Prefer concrete, defensible claims over vague inspiration. Claims about relationships should be testable against behavior, consequences, role reversal, power, consent, trust, repair, and long-term responsibility.

The repo-local Codex skill for this work lives at [.agents/skills/fidelity-writing](.agents/skills/fidelity-writing). Keep Fidelity-specific Codex skills in this repository rather than installing them globally.

# Review Workflow

When reviewing or revising a chapter with the user, proceed paragraph by paragraph. Present one paragraph at a time, wait for the user's edits or approval, apply edits to that paragraph, then move to the next paragraph in the chapter.

Do not batch-review an entire chapter unless the user explicitly asks for a full-pass review. The default review method for this book is interactive paragraph-level editing.
