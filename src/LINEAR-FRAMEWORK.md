# Fidelity Linear Framework

The Fidelity Framework moves from the nature of human bonds to the public and generational consequences of trustworthy love.

## Phase One: Define Trustworthy Bonds

The reader begins by recognizing that fidelity is more than emotion, possession, or social status. It is a pattern of truthful, reliable, bounded, repairable love.

Chapters: 01-06.

## Phase Two: Order Intimate Commitments

The reader then considers dating, sexuality, marriage, family, caregiving across dependence, and conflict. These domains require embodied responsibility because desire, power, vulnerability, and time are involved.

Chapters: 07-12.

## Phase Three: Repair And Protect

The reader applies fidelity to apology, forgiveness, boundaries, betrayal, estrangement, vulnerability, and friendship across difference. Trust must be repaired truthfully and protected from exploitation.

Chapters: 13-18.

## Phase Four: Endure Across Time

The final movement concerns desire, attachment, technology, community support, public norms, grief, and the faithful life. The test is whether love remains defensible across years, decades, and generations.

Chapters: 19-24.

## Operating Cadence

Use this cadence when applying the framework:

1. Name the bond or relational pattern.
2. Identify what conduct it actually produces.
3. Reverse roles with the person most affected.
4. Identify what trust is being built or broken.
5. Identify what boundary is needed.
6. Name what needs repair or protection.
7. Choose one faithful practice.
8. Review what the pattern becomes over time.
